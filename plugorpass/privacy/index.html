PlugOrPass Privacy Policy

This policy explains what PlugOrPass stores, what it transmits off-device, and when those transfers occur. It is based on the current app implementation reviewed for this disclosure.

Summary

PlugOrPass primarily stores your comparison data on your device. The app does not appear to include third-party ad SDKs or behavioral advertising trackers in the current codebase reviewed for this disclosure.

Your data is sent off-device only for specific features you choose to use, including optional location lookup, VIN decoding, fuel economy lookups, optional AI autofill, optional Sign in with Apple plus iCloud sync, optional comparison sharing, App Store subscription processing, and feedback emails you decide to send.

If you do not use optional cloud, AI, or sharing features, PlugOrPass operates mostly with local storage plus the vehicle and pricing lookup requests needed to calculate results.

At a Glance

Data stored locally

Saved comparisons, addresses, user profile inputs, selected AI settings, flagged AI results, crash reports waiting to be sent, and free-comparison counters.

Sensitive inputs

Address details, optional location coordinates, VINs, vehicle selections, ownership assumptions, insurance profile inputs, and anything you include in AI prompts or support emails.

Optional cloud storage

Saved comparisons can sync to your private iCloud database after Sign in with Apple. Shared comparison codes use CloudKit public storage and expire after about 24 hours.

Information PlugOrPass Collects

Information you enter

Street, city, state, ZIP code, vehicle details, VINs, ownership years, mileage, fuel prices, insurance assumptions, maintenance costs, tire data, saved comparisons, and any feedback or support messages you write.

Approximate or device-derived data

If you grant location permission, the app can request your current location and convert it into an address and coordinates for localized pricing and comparison inputs.

Account and subscription data

If you use Sign in with Apple, the app stores your Apple user identifier in Keychain and may store the name and email Apple provides. Subscription status is handled through Apple StoreKit.

AI configuration data

If you enable AI tools, your chosen provider and model settings are stored locally. API keys are stored in iOS Keychain rather than plain local preferences.

How Data Is Used

To build and save vehicle ownership comparisons.
To localize fuel and electricity pricing and geocode addresses.
To decode VINs and retrieve public vehicle economy data.
To provide optional AI-assisted autofill and estimation features.
To sync saved comparisons through your private iCloud account if you enable that feature.
To process subscriptions and restore purchases through Apple.
To create temporary share codes when you explicitly share a comparison.
To help diagnose bugs if you choose to send feedback with logs or crash information attached.

Local Storage and Retention

Saved comparisons are stored in a local SQLite database under the app's Application Support directory. User profile settings, feature counters, AI model preferences, flagged AI results, and pending crash logs are stored locally in app preferences. API keys and the Apple user identifier are stored in iOS Keychain.

Data remains on the device until you delete it, clear the app, remove items inside the app, uninstall the app, or overwrite local records. Shared comparison codes in CloudKit public storage are intended to expire roughly 24 hours after creation.

Children and Sensitive Data

PlugOrPass is a vehicle cost comparison tool and is not designed specifically for children. The app is not intended to collect special categories of sensitive personal data beyond the information users choose to enter for comparison, account, support, and optional AI features.

You should avoid including unnecessary personal information in AI prompts, feedback emails, shared comparisons, or free-form notes.

Your Choices

You can decline location permission and enter an address manually.

You can avoid Sign in with Apple and use the app without iCloud sync.

You can avoid AI features entirely by not adding API keys and not sending AI prompts.

You can choose not to generate share codes and not to send feedback emails or crash logs.

You can contact support to request help understanding or deleting data under your control.

Security

The current implementation stores API keys and the Apple user identifier in Keychain rather than plain text settings. Other content such as saved comparisons and preferences is stored using standard iOS app storage mechanisms.

No method of storage or transmission is perfectly secure. If you believe there is a privacy or security issue, contact veloralabssupport@gmail.com.
